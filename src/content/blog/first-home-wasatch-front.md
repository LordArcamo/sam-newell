Everyone walks into their first showing staring at one number: the price. After fifteen years of Utah deals — and a construction management degree that taught me to see through drywall — I can tell you the price is the *fifth* most important number on the sheet. Here are the five that matter more, in order.

## 1. Your monthly payment at today's rate

Not the price — the payment. A $450K house at one rate and a $480K house at another can cost the same per month. Get pre-approved first, know your real payment ceiling, and shop payments, not prices. This is also why a strong lending partner matters: faster approval means stronger offers.

## 2. The age of the roof, furnace, and water heater

These are the three most expensive surprises in a first home, and none of them show up in the listing photos. A roof has maybe 20–25 good years in Utah's freeze-thaw cycle; a furnace 15–20; a water heater 10–12. Add up what's left on each. That number belongs in your offer math.

## 3. Days on market — for the neighborhood, not the house

One house sitting for 40 days might be overpriced. A whole neighborhood averaging 40 days tells you who has the leverage — and it's you. Your agent should pull this number before you write anything.

## 4. The framing behind the finishes

I framed houses before I sold them, and here's the truth: paint is $40 a gallon, but bones are forever. Squeaky floors, doors that don't close square, cracks radiating from window corners — these are the house telling you about its structure. Listen to it before you fall in love with the kitchen.

## 5. What it rents for

Even if you never plan to move, know the rent number. It's your escape hatch (life happens), your upgrade path (first homes make great first rentals), and the fastest way to tell whether you're buying a home *and* an asset — or just a home.
